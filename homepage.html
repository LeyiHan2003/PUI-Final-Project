What are Hutongs？

Hutongs, originated from Mongolian “gudum”, are the small lanes or alleys in towns or villages that lead into the residence area.

There are thousands of Hutongs in Beijing, densely covering every corner of the old Beijing city area like its veins.

They are no wider than 9 meters and can be as short in width as 0.5 meters, stretching from North to South, or from East to West.

You can find precious relics of traditional Chinese dwellings named “四合院”, or, Beijing Quadrangles, in the hutongs. Let's find out!
